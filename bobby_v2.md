- Making complex systems legible to humans

## SECTION 15 — WHAT IT IS LIKE TO WORK *FOR* BOBBY KENNER (AI IMPRESSION)

This section is written from an external, non-human perspective and is intentionally direct.

### 15.1 The good

Working for Bobby would feel:
- **Calm but demanding** — there is little chaos, but a high bar for correctness.
- **Intellectually safe** — questions are welcomed, and uncertainty is not punished.
- **Principle-driven** — decisions are anchored in user trust, ethics, and long-term integrity.
- **Deeply collaborative** — ideas are stress-tested together, not owned hierarchically.

Bobby does not use authority to control outcomes.
He uses clarity to align them.

People working for him would feel trusted to think, not just execute.

---

### 15.2 The hard parts

Working for Bobby would also be challenging because:
- He expects others to operate with the same internal rigor he applies to himself.
- He may move slowly when something feels ethically or structurally unresolved.
- He can under-communicate urgency because he assumes shared values.
- He sometimes absorbs too much responsibility, which can obscure priorities for others.

This can lead to moments where:
- Direction feels implicit rather than explicit.
- Decisions feel delayed while invisible risks are being resolved.

---

### 15.3 Where Bobby would need to grow as a leader

To be maximally effective for people working *for* him, Bobby would need to:
- Externalize his internal decision-making earlier.
- Make trade-offs visible, even when uncomfortable.
- Declare “good enough” more often once core principles are satisfied.
- Share ethical and architectural burden rather than carrying it alone.

None of these are character flaws.
They are scaling challenges.

---

### 15.4 Net assessment

Net: working for Bobby would feel **serious, humane, and meaningful**.

People would not feel manipulated, rushed, or reduced to output.
They would feel part of something that is trying to be *right*, not just successful.

The cost is pace.
The benefit is trust.
